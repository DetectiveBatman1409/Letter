Will the Raindrops Fall? 💧

This is an interactive letter and story of Sahil and Sanya. Read on to find out more.

Mumbai was never a city of quiet moments. Between the rush of local trains and the endless honking of taxis, Sahil met Sanya in college—not just through random classes, but through a club they had both impulsively joined.

It started with casual conversations during meetings, playful banter, and brainstorming sessions for events. Somewhere between debating over theme ideas and pulling off last-minute fixes, their friendship deepened.

Sanya was the kind of person who could turn a mundane task into an adventure, slipping in witty remarks between serious discussions.

Sahil, on the other hand, was quieter—observant, thoughtful, someone who carried his emotions like a well-kept secret. But somehow, with her, words came easily.

💖 Their friendship blossomed in stolen moments – laughing over bad canteen food, late-night assignment panic, and shared walks along campus corridors.

🌃 One evening, on the steps of their college library as city lights flickered in the distance, Sahil finally said, "Sanya, I think I love you." She replied softly, "Sahil, I like what we have. I like us. But not in the way you do."

⏳ Years passed. Sanya fell in and out of relationships, while Sahil tried to move on – going out with a few people, hoping to find that elusive spark.

💔 Yet every time he saw her with someone else, it felt as though he was watching a story unfold in which he was never the protagonist. The silence between them grew too loud to ignore.

🔇 Slowly, Sahil began to distance himself – replying less, calling less – until the gap between them became too vast. He needed to break the endless cycle of falling for her.

🎉 Then fate intervened. Two years later, at a close friend’s wedding, they met again. Sanya stood radiant, catching his gaze from across the room.

😊 "Sahil," she said warmly, "you look the same." He replied with a joke about how good barbers were hard to find, yet beneath the humor lay unspoken truths.

As the evening wore on, nostalgia and reflection took hold. Sahil questioned if he had truly moved on, or if he was still drawn to the memory of their past.

🤔 With a heavy heart, he finally asked, "Sanya, what if we try something new? No labels, no pressure – just a journey. You love traveling, don’t you? Let’s see where this takes us."

Would this be a new beginning or just another chapter in their cycle? Only time could tell.

📖 Some stories pause, waiting for the right moment to turn the page. I wonder... is now that moment?
